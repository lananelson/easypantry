## Ingredients

- 2 cups (260g) all-purpose flour (crust)
- 1 tsp salt (crust)
- 2/3 cup (150g) chilled unsalted butter, cubed (crust)
- 5 tbsp (75ml) cold water (crust)
- 1 tbsp (14g) butter (filling)
- 1 shallot, peeled and diced
- 1 small bunch (200g) asparagus, stemmed and cut into 1.5-inch pieces
- 4 large eggs
- 3/4 cup (180ml) milk (2% or whole)
- 1/4 cup (60ml) heavy cream
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1/4 tsp cayenne
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp chopped parsley
- 125g soft unripened goat cheese, crumbled

## Instructions

1. Mix flour, salt, and butter until pea-sized bits remain. Add cold water gradually until dough forms. Wrap and chill 30+ minutes.
2. Roll dough to a 12-inch circle, transfer to a greased 9-inch pie dish, trim edges, and poke bottom with a fork.
3. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C).
4. Melt butter in a pan and sauté diced shallot until lightly caramelized, about 6–7 minutes. Set aside.
5. Boil asparagus pieces for 4 minutes until slightly firm; drain.
6. Beat eggs, milk, cream, Dijon, cayenne, salt, pepper, and parsley together. Stir in goat cheese and cooked shallots.
7. Scatter asparagus in the crust. Pour the egg mixture over top, distributing the cheese evenly.
8. Bake for 55 minutes until crust is golden and filling is set.
9. Cool at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Notes

- Asparagus should remain slightly firm after boiling — it continues cooking in the oven.
- Can be served warm, at room temperature, or chilled.
